AI Discovery Interview

Answer each question as the owner would respond. The AI generates the action plan at the end.

Your goal: Find 1–2 specific workflow problems where AI can make a visible difference within 2 weeks. The right answer to each question is a specific story, not a general opinion. When in doubt: "tell me about the last time that happened" gets you further than "we have some issues with X."
Phase 1 — Time
If you had an extra hour every day — not on client work, not on admin — where would you put it?
What they wish they could do if time weren't the constraint.
What's the one task you do over and over that you wish just handled itself?
The repetitive, low-value work that eats energy.
When was the last time you or your team spent more than 30 minutes on something that could have been automated?
Recent, concrete examples that ground the conversation in reality.
Phase 2 — Workflow
Walk me through how a job goes from first call or email to completed work. Where does it slow down?
Where the bottlenecks are — often at handoffs between people or systems.
What information do you wish you had right now that you don't have easy access to?
Reporting and visibility gaps. What would they like to know at a glance?
What do you have to tell customers "I'll get back to you on that" that you could look up yourself right now?
Information access problems. What do they chase down repeatedly?
Phase 3 — Communication
How do you handle customer follow-up right now? Does it happen consistently?
Nurture and follow-up gaps. Are quotes followed up on? Reminders sent?
What percentage of your emails or messages are pretty much the same thing over and over?
Template and response automation potential.
Do you ever lose track of where a customer is in the process — like forgetting to send an invoice or confirm an appointment?
Tracking and reminder gaps.
Phase 4 — Quick Wins
If I handed you a tool tomorrow that solved one problem — just one — what would you want it to do?
The most pressing pain. The thing they think about at 11pm when they're catching up on admin.
What's the most annoying part of your week that you just accept as "part of the job?"
Learned helplessness. Things they've stopped trying to fix. Often the highest-value targets.
Phase 5 — Reality Check
Do you have your customer list, job history, and pricing stored somewhere a computer could read?
Data readiness. Software, clean spreadsheets, or is it all on paper and in people's heads?
How do you feel about AI tools right now — excited, skeptical, overwhelmed?
Owner mindset. Shapes how you frame every recommendation.
Business type, size, and anything else worth knowing?
Context that helps tailor the recommendations.
